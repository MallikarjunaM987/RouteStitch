PROBLEM STATEMENT:
Travelers across India face significant challenges when planning intercity journeys requiring multiple transport modes. Existing platforms are fragmented — train, bus, flight, and local transport information are scattered across different apps, with no unified system to coordinate connections, compare costs, or plan first-mile and last-mile travel efficiently. This leads to wasted time, missed connections, and higher travel costs.

- Over 50 million intercity travelers move daily in India
- 86% of trains sold out within 10 days
- No single platform currently links IRCTC, state/private buses, airlines, and local transport
- City-level solutions exist (Tummoc, Chennai-One) but no national solution
- 40% higher costs due to poor coordination
- 25% of travelers miss connections due to fragmentation
